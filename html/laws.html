Minnesota Laws

Minnesota’s minimm goal for the wolf population is 1,600, which the current population is well above. The U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service has declared that wolves are no longer threatened in Minnesota, Wisconsin, and Michgan. However, courts have rejected the service’s attemps to take the region’s wolves off of the endangered list. This means that as long as grey wolves are on the list, it is illegal to kill them except to protect human life. Currently, Minnesota is rallying in Congress to return wolves to atate management, and not federal jurisdiction.
