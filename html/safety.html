Wolf Safety

Wolf attacks on humans are really rare, but if you run into a wolf, raise your arms to make yourself seem bigger, and make a lot of noise; never turn your back to the wolf and back away slowly. Use pepper spray if you need to.

To avoid encounters with wolves: keep a clean camp, pack garbage and not bury it, always keep an eye on children, choose a camp near restrrom facilities, do not camp near a carcass, wash dishes in a container and dispose of the dirty water in a body of water far away from camp. Do not approach wolves ever.

There are animals that are half-wolf and half-dog. In Minnesota, it is illegal to own an animal that is a domestic/wild hybrid. However, there are sancuaries for wolf-dog hybrids.
